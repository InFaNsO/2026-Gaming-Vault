# Dream Game — Pitch

> **Locked vision (2026-06-06).** Supersedes the earlier "medieval kingdom colonizes a magic island" pitch. Set in the **Mani world** (same world as the [[00 Looter Shooter|Looter Shooter]], a *different era*, **no plot connection** — callbacks only).

## Elevator pitch

A man who **accidentally became immortal** is on a conquest to expand his kingdom. He discovers a **Bermuda-Triangle-like zone** where mysterious phenomena occur, sails in, and **colonizes a mysterious island.** The island is riddled with **portals / dungeons** (Solo-Leveling-style) holding **beasts** and **otherworldly tech that can advance his civilization to the next era.** He runs **one persistent Main City** (his mainland home) and a smaller **Satellite City** on the island, and delves the dungeons for power and progress.

## The core threat (the roguelike engine)

**Failing to finish a dungeon in time → its beasts break out of the portal and run loose on the island, destroying it.** The player has two answers:
1. **Clear the dungeon** (offense), or
2. **Build enough island defenses** to stop the escaping beasts (defense).

If the player **fails**, the **island (Satellite) city is destroyed**, the player **re-animates in the Main City**, and does it all over again — **bigger**, keeping persistent tech/knowledge. That cycle is the roguelike.

## The four pillars + how they interact

- **Looter-shooter (combat)** — the dungeon delves. **Loot = getting stronger + advancing the city.** (Reuses the Looter-Shooter combat; modifiable for the fusion.)
- **Automation** — **Mani spell-engineering**: build spells *inside* Mani gems (Modulus-style, particle-system magic); gem size = build space. This is your dungeon power.
- **Roguelike** — **re-animation** + storytelling delivered **through the dungeons** + the mystery of the Triangle.
- **City-building (Anno-style)** — manage the civilization's **needs** and **resource transfers between towns** (Main City ↔ Satellite City logistics).

## Persistent vs. run

- **Persistent (meta):** the **Main City**, tech, knowledge — they survive every island's fall.
- **The run:** the **Satellite island city** + its open dungeons — risky, can be lost entirely.

## Mani-world grounding (callbacks, not continuity)

- **The portals are interdimensional gates** — the same *kind* of phenomenon as **The Breach** (a doorway to elsewhere; otherworldly things come through).
- **The immortality is the Husk-making force with the mind intact** — the thing that hollows others into mindless immortals, but he kept *himself*. (The "…oh, like the Husks" nod.)
- **Otherworldly beasts + tech = Akashic.** Same cosmic-horror well, a new era.

## Open design decisions (to finalize next)

1. **The dungeon-break clock** (the keystone) — how gates tick toward breaking; juggling multiple open dungeons at once (triage: clear / defend / sacrifice).
2. **What persists / extracts** — Tarkov-style: ship loot home *before* the island falls, or lose it.
3. **Clear-vs-defend must be a real dilemma** — only *clearing* yields the era-advancing otherworldly tech, so you're *forced* to push (defense = survival, offense = progress).
4. **Keep the Main City engaging**, not a static menu (the Anno heartland the satellite depends on).
5. **Run granularity** — how long a typical run is, what carries over.
6. **Testbed → fusion roadmap** (task 5b) — which smaller games prove which pillar, in what order, and how each folds in (no scope cuts to the dream game; testbeds are the de-risking strategy).
