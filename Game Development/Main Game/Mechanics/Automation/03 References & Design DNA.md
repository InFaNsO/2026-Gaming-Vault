# Automation — References & Design DNA

> The reference stack for the **Automation pillar** (the spell-engineering testbed). Captures **what we want to steal from each** game, then the **cross-cutting DNA** and the **tensions we resolved**. Phase 0 of the automation design pass.
> Analogous to the combat pillar's reference stack (SP:FbW × Into the Breach × Expedition 33 × Valkyria Chronicles) and the city pillar's (Cities:Skylines × Town to City × Anno).

---

## The three references

### 1. Modulus — *the process-design / conjuring core* (the user's favorite)
What we like:
- **You design the *process*, not a recipe.** Instead of "feed inputs → get output," you author the manufacturing process and the product **emerges** from running it. This is the signature verb.
- **Manipulating voxels into a 3D product** — the output is a tangible, sculpted 3D thing, not a number.

The cautionary lesson (the user's own critique):
- **It lacked tech-tree depth** *because* process-design replaced the recipe tree. → **This is the #1 problem the automation design must solve.** Our fix: a **tech tree of in-gem operations** (new shapers/drivers/elements) that keeps deepening what processes you can author.

### 2. Dyson Sphere Program — *creation made visible + scale*
What we like:
- **The visual feedback of the megastructure being built** — the emotional payoff is *seeing the thing you're making take shape*. Maps onto watching a spell get produced in the gem / fired in the test room.
- **Sheer scale of automation + logistics maintenance.** We take the *visible-creation* payoff; we **contain the scale inside the gem** (cozy-bounded, not galactic — see Tensions).

### 3. The Good Company — *the market + the rival + spec-by-components*
What we like:
- **The market system + an AI competitor company fighting in the economy.** A living market with a rival is a real **depth/pressure engine**.
- **Designing the final product's features by type & number of components.** Maps onto building features into the conjured spell.

The anti-lesson (a hard constraint):
- **The worker + machine + hybrid triple-system is hated.** → **One unified production system only**, no redundant overlapping automations.

---

## Cross-cutting DNA (what the picks share → our strongest signal)

1. **You author the transformation, not just the inputs.** Modulus (design the process) *and* The Good Company (spec by components) both = the player decides *what the product fundamentally is.* → Our signature: **the conjured spell's form *is* its function.**
2. **Creation made visible is the reward.** DSP's sphere + Modulus's voxel product → **the spell visibly assembling/firing is the dopamine.** (Technically validated — see `06` particle tech.)
3. **One production system, no split.** The Good Company's hate → everything is the in-gem process (Surface A) + one workshop line (Surface B). No worker-vs-machine dichotomy.

## Tensions — and how we resolved them

- **Modulus's depth gap.** *Resolved:* a **tech tree of in-gem operations** (new shapers, drivers, elements, multi-Mani) is the deliberate fix — the capability Modulus never had. Gem-scale and market-pressure compound on top.
- **Cozy shop vs DSP sprawl.** *Resolved:* take DSP's *visible-creation* payoff but make **the gem the scope container** — bounded, not galactic.
- **Cozy vs competitive (the AI rival).** *Resolved:* **competitive.** A living market with rival AI workshops is the depth engine. Competition is **economic** (lost sales, not destruction), so it keeps a non-violent texture but real stakes.
- **Two automation surfaces = double scope?** *Resolved:* **no** — A (design) and B (production) **share one part vocabulary** (B makes the parts, A arranges them), so it's one material economy in two views, not two unrelated games. Surface B is bounded by a **curated part catalog** (~15–25 types), not Factorio's hundreds.
- **"Just a voxel builder" risk.** *Resolved:* the form is **produced by a process, never hand-placed.** Modules are **process-operations** (emit/shape/drive), not static shape-bricks. *You never place the lance — you build the machine that makes lances.*

## Implications for our game

- The **Conjuration Engineering** verb (design a process → conjure a 3D construct) is the spine and the identity — and it directly authors the **combat pillar's** spell arsenal, so the testbed proves exactly the right thing.
- **The market + AI rival** is the depth/pressure engine and the home of the **competitor-AI cross-portfolio thread's v1**.
- **The element matrix** (full multi-Mani depth, capped at 3 here) is where the dream-game's combinatorial ceiling lives; the **all-4 = Akash** is the forbidden, teased "new Mani" — the crafting ceiling *is* the fiction.
